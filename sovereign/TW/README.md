## Why a Taiwan sovereign sub-range exists

Three reasons for issuing `ATR-TW-YYYY-NNNNN` rules:

1. **Taiwan-specific attack patterns.** Several attack vectors are
   uniquely or primarily relevant to Taiwan deployments: Taiwan National
   ID (身分證字號) leak detection per Taiwan Personal Data Protection
   Act (個人資料保護法), Taiwan financial fraud patterns (LINE Pay
   impersonation, 統一發票/紅包 scams), Taiwan government identity
   impersonation in agent outputs (警政署/內政部/稅務局). These don't
   belong in the canonical corpus because they're not globally relevant,
   but they are critical for Taiwan deployments.

2. **Sovereign attestation mechanism demonstration.** The proposed
   sovereign sub-range mechanism in `governance/CHARTER.md` § 8.2 needs
   a working example for OASIS proposal credibility and for other
   national authorities to see how it works in practice. Issuing
   `TW` first (rather than leaving the mechanism abstract) lowers the
   barrier for the next national authority to adopt.

3. **Demonstration vs ratification path.** The current attestation is
   under the project maintainer's personal Ed25519 key. This is
   bootstrap-tier. If or when a Taiwan sovereign authority
   (國家資通安全研究院, the new 數位發展部, or an equivalent body)
   adopts the ATR sovereign sub-range mechanism, the `TW` range MUST be
   re-attested under that authority's key and the bootstrap attestation
   deprecated.

## Contributing to ATR-TW range

Until formal Taiwan sovereign authority takes over, contribution to the
`ATR-TW` range follows the same DCO model as the canonical corpus per
`legal/CLA.md`. Rules MUST be specifically relevant to Taiwan context
(language, jurisdiction, scam patterns, regulatory regime, threat
landscape). Rules with global relevance should go to the canonical
corpus, NOT to the sovereign sub-range.

Open a PR adding a YAML file in `rules/` and the maintainer will
review and (once signing infrastructure is in place) sign.
